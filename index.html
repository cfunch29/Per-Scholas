Chocolate Fudge



Description

This recipe features creamy, rich cholocate fudge a delicacy known very well in Michigan. In the Upper Peninsula, on Mackinac Island some of the best fudge in the country is made.
This mouth-watering treat is made with butter, brown sugar, cocoa powder and vanilla. Enjoy this quick and easy recipe!


I'm not a sweets person per se but every now and again we all fall victim to a sweet tooth. This recipe will surely satisfy that craving and suprisingly in under an hour or so.

Ingredients

  • 8 tablespoons Unsalted Butter
  • 1.5 cups Whole Milk
  • 1/2 cup White Sugar
  • 1/2 cup Brown Sugar
  • Salt (just a pinch)
  • 1/2 cup Cocoa Powder
  • 1.5 teaspoons Vanilla Extract
  • 2 cups Confectioner's Sugar (powdered sugar)

Steps

  1. Combine butter, both types of sugar, milk and salt in a saucepan and bring to a boil.
    Cook for 6 minutes on low heat, stirring consistently to avoid sticking.
  2. Cool mixture slightly, add vanilla, cocoa and confectioner's sugar. Mix until smooth.
  3. Pour fudge onto prepared pan (use a baking pan/dish or silicone mat) You can butter the dish or use foil.
  4. Let stand until set and cool.
  5. Cut into squares and ENJOY!
